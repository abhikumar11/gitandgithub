The Stationery Shop of Tehran by Marjan Kamali is a historical romance set against one of the most turbulent periods in modern Iranian history.

Beginning
In 1953 Tehran, Roya, a 17-year-old girl who loves books and poetry, frequently visits a charming stationery shop owned by Mr. Fakhri. The shop serves as a gathering place for readers, dreamers, and intellectuals.
There she meets Bahman Aslan, a passionate young man with strong ideals about justice and a love for Persian literature. They quickly fall in love and become engaged.

The Turning Point
Just before their wedding, political unrest grips Iran. Demonstrations, violence, and uncertainty fill the streets.
Roya waits for Bahman at their agreed meeting place before they are to leave together—but he never arrives.
Heartbroken and believing she has been abandoned, Roya eventually leaves Iran.
